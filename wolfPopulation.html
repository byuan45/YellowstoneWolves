Wolf Populations

Wolves are both highly social and territorial. They formed their packs immediately, and the packs are essential for hunting and overall survival. The packs are often aggressive towards each other when they must protect each other and defend territory or an animal carcass. Because of this aggression, wolves often die during interactions with those outside of their own pack. This can cause fluctuations in pack sizes, even to the point of a pack dying out or a new one beginning.
