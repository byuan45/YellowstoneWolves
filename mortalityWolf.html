Mortality of Wolves

Even though the wolves are top predators, they can die for multiple reasons. “Intraspecific” is the categorization of a death resulting from competition within the wolf species. This is generally the most common mortality cause, but there have been other reasons in the recent history of the wolves in Yellowstone. Wolves can die due to competition with other species as well, or just of natural causes. Additionally, there was an outbreak of a viral disease in 2005, 2008, and 2009, and sarcoptic mange hit the wolves in epidemic proportions in 2009, which caused a large reduction in the population.
